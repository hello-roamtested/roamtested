**Cheapest per GB overall:** NXTL and Yesim Pay & Fly for light users; Nomad's Europe 50GB for heavy users. **Watch out:** "unlimited" plans always carry fair-use caps — Yesim throttles after ~50GB/30 days; Nomad's unlimited gives 2GB/day at full speed.

## Coverage & Networks

**Airalo** partners with named local carriers per plan (shown before purchase), e.g., China Unicom in China; 5G on many plans. **Nomad** lists named carriers too, often dual-network (e.g., China Unicom + Telecom). **Yesim** connects to multiple networks per country — in the US it uses T-Mobile and AT&T simultaneously, auto-selecting the stronger signal. **TooSim** rides AT&T/T-Mobile/Verizon in the US and Deutsche Telekom/Vodafone/Orange in Europe, but doesn't always disclose the carrier pre-purchase. **NXTL** is unique: you can manually pick among networks in a country to get a cheaper per-GB rate.

## Which One Should You Pick?

- **First-time eSIM user / want the safest choice:** Airalo — biggest catalog, mature app, solid support.
- **Best all-rounder on price + features:** Nomad — free hotspot everywhere, named carriers, free {N.trial_gb}GB trial to test before paying.
- **Light user or unpredictable itinerary:** NXTL or Yesim Pay & Fly — pay only for what you use, no expiring bundles.
- **Heavy data user:** Nomad's 50GB Europe plan or Yesim's unlimited passes (mind the FUP).
- **TooSim:** competitive in the US and Europe (from ${T.usa_per_gb_from}/GB), long-validity plans up to 180 days — but compare per-destination, as some markets (e.g., China) are priced far above rivals, and there's no plan top-up.
