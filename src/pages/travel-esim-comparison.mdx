## Trip.com eSIM — the travel-platform option

Trip.com sells eSIMs alongside flights and hotels, which makes it structurally different from every dedicated brand above — and for China specifically, it's the only one here sold through the **three official domestic carriers** (China Mobile, Unicom, Telecom) rather than as foreign roaming. Traffic still routes internationally, which is why blocked apps are reported to work without a VPN.

**Where it wins:** flexibility and risk. The 1-to-365-day ladder means you're not forced into a 7/15/30-day box, and being able to cancel right up until installation is something no dedicated eSIM brand here offers.

**Where to be careful:** no fair-use policy is published before purchase — neither the daily high-speed cap nor the throttled speed. And while it's marketed as "ChatGPT available," at least one buyer reports ChatGPT still requiring a VPN even though Google, Instagram, Facebook and WhatsApp worked. We're verifying both points in field testing.

## Coverage & Networks

**Airalo** partners with named local carriers per plan (shown before purchase), e.g., China Unicom in China; 5G on many plans. **Nomad** lists named carriers too, often dual-network (e.g., China Unicom + Telecom). **Yesim** connects to multiple networks per country — in the US it uses T-Mobile and AT&T simultaneously, auto-selecting the stronger signal. **TooSim** rides AT&T/T-Mobile/Verizon in the US and Deutsche Telekom/Vodafone/Orange in Europe, but doesn't always disclose the carrier pre-purchase. **NXTL** is unique: you can manually pick among networks in a country to get a cheaper per-GB rate. **Trip.com** discloses its carriers upfront for China — all three state operators.

## Which One Should You Pick?

- **First-time eSIM user / want the safest choice:** Airalo — biggest catalog, mature app, solid support.
- **Best all-rounder on price + features:** Nomad — free hotspot everywhere, named carriers, free {N.trial_gb}GB trial to test before paying.
- **Light user or unpredictable itinerary:** NXTL or Yesim Pay & Fly — pay only for what you use, no expiring bundles.
- **Heavy data user:** Nomad's 50GB Europe plan or Yesim's unlimited passes (mind the FUP).
- **Trip transiting China, or an itinerary that might change:** Trip.com — the 1-to-365-day ladder and refund-before-install policy give you the most room to change your mind, and it's the only option here running on official Chinese carriers.
- **TooSim:** competitive in the US and Europe (from ${T.usa_per_gb_from}/GB), long-validity plans up to 180 days — but compare per-destination, as some markets (e.g., China) are priced far above rivals, and there's no plan top-up.
